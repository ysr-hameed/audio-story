# PHASE 3: STORY ARCHITECTURE

**Purpose:** Plan the complete narrative structure with acts, addiction loop, consequences, and twist.

**Prerequisites:** Phase 1 and Phase 2 completed.

---

## THREE-ACT STORY TEMPLATE

### ACT 1: ESTABLISHMENT (~2,000 words)

**Phase 1 — Hook + Emotional Backstory (first 300 words = HOOK, then 1,000 words):**

⚠️ **CRITICAL: The hook (mystery/discovery) MUST appear within the first 300 words. Do NOT delay it.**

**Structure:**
- **Lines 1-5:** Hook — protagonist encounters something strange (the mechanism, the mystery, the first hint)
- **Lines 6-300:** Backstory woven into the hook — WHO is this person, WHY are they here, what is their routine
- **Lines 300-1,000:** Deepen character + first denial/dismissal of the mystery

```
❌ WRONG: 1,000 words of backstory → THEN mystery appears
✅ RIGHT: Hook in first 300 words → THEN backstory woven around it
```

- Cover all 6 backstory template points ABOVE the mystery
- Include 2-3 specific memories that reveal character
- Show routine + atmosphere in detail
- Include a minor frustration or worry (not mystery-related)

**Phase 2 — First Hint (~500 words):**
- The mystery element arrives or is discovered
- Protagonist dismisses it as coincidence or mistake
- First denial: "ये तो कुछ और ही होगा।"
- Keep the tone curious, not scared yet
- Show protagonist's analytical mind working

**Phase 3 — Testing (~500 words):**
- Something happens that CAN'T be coincidence
- Protagonist actively TESTS the mechanism
- First fear: "मेरे रोंगटे खड़े हो गए।"
- Protagonist looks for rational explanations
- Second denial: "कोई मजाक कर रहा है।"

---

### ACT 2: ESCALATION (~4,000 words)

**Phase 4 — Mechanism Shown 4-5 Times with Variation & Consequences:**

Use the mechanism variation table from Phase 2. Each showing must include:
- Different delivery or trigger
- Darker/more personal content
- Rising stakes
- External consequence (someone else involved, physical evidence found, authority contacted)

**Phase 5 — Supporting Characters (3 characters, each with a role):**

Each must have:
- A NAME (not just "the guard")
- A personality quirk
- A specific viewpoint about the protagonist
- A moment where they react to the mystery
- **MINIMUM 2-3 SCENES with dialogue**

```
❌ WEAK: Character is in the template but never has a scene.
   "अनीता नर्स है। वो काम करती है।"
   → She's a NAME, not a character. The listener never sees her.

✅ STRONG: Character has 2-3 SCENES with dialogue.
   Scene 1: "अनीता बोली — विजय जी, आप आज अजीब लग रहे हैं।"
   Scene 2: "अनीता ने पूछा — सर, वो शीशी क्यों हिल रही है?"
   Scene 3: "अनीता बोली — मैं डर गई हूं। मुझे यहां से जाना है।"
   → She's a REAL person. The listener sees her grow from curious to scared.
```

**Phase 6 — Moral Dilemma (500-700 words):**

The protagonist must face a choice where BOTH options have CLEAR, TANGIBLE costs:

| Option A | Option B |
|----------|----------|
| Follow the mechanism's instructions | Ignore the mechanism |
| ✅ Save your reputation BUT someone gets hurt | ✅ Save someone BUT lose your job/reputation |
| ✅ Tell the truth BUT lose everything | ✅ Stay silent BUT live with guilt |

**Structure:**
1. Present the two options clearly
2. Show protagonist weighing costs (internal debate, 3-4 sentences)
3. Physical reaction (pacing, sweating, staring at ceiling)
4. Make the choice
5. Immediate consequence of that choice

```
❌ WEAK: "मुझे फाइल पढ़नी चाहिए या नहीं?" (abstract, no tangible cost)
✅ STRONG: "अगर मैं पुलिस को बताता हूं तो मेरी नौकरी जाएगी। 55 साल की उम्र में मुझे कोई और नौकरी नहीं देगा। लेकिन अगर नहीं बताता तो वो आदमी और लोगों को मारता रहेगा। मैं इसके साथ नहीं जी सकता।"
```

---

### ACT 3: CLIMAX (~2,000 words)

**Phase 7 — The Twist:**
- Reframes EVERYTHING the listener thought they knew
- Must be FORESHADOWED (clues in phases 1-6)
- Must make listener think "OH SHIT"
- Must NOT be: "it was all a dream", "I have a split personality", "it was me all along"

```
✅ GOOD TWISTS from examples:
- The letter-writer IS the serial killer (clue: detailed crime knowledge)
- The painting shows the future AND creates it (clue: arm wound appeared after painting)
```

**Phase 8 — Confrontation:**
- Protagonist faces the truth directly
- Show tangible, physical consequences
- Ending that LINGERS — not neatly resolved
- Final 2-3 lines should be haunting

## THE ADDICTION LOOP

Every phase MUST be VISIBLE in the text with the protagonist's exact words:

| Phase | Feel | Hindi Text Required | Context |
|-------|------|-------------------|---------|
| 1. Curiosity | "What is this?" | "अरे ये क्या है? ये तो अजीब बात है।" | First discovery |
| 2. Excitement | "This is amazing!" | "वाह! ये तो कमाल है। ये सच में हो रहा है।" | First confirmation |
| 3. Greed | "I want more." | "मुझे और चाहिए। बस एक बार और देख लूं। उसके बाद छोड़ दूंगा।" | After 2-3 repetitions |
| 4. Fear | "Something is wrong." | "अब डर लगने लगा है। ये सही नहीं है। मुझे रुक जाना चाहिए।" | When content gets personal |
| 5. Guilt | "I shouldn't." | "मुझे पता है ये गलत है। लेकिन मैं रुक नहीं पा रहा।" | Before/after moral dilemma |
| 6. Denial | "It's fine." | "ये सिर्फ संयोग है। मैं बेवजह डर रहा हूं। कुछ नहीं होगा।" | After each exit opportunity |
| 7. Addiction | "Can't stop." | "मैं रुक नहीं सकता। मुझे जानना है। अब और नहीं रुक सकता।" | Mid-act 2 |
| 8. Panic | "I'm trapped." | "अब मैं बहुत गहरे फंस चुका हूं। कोई रास्ता नहीं है।" | End of act 2 / act 3 |

**⚠️ Warning: Excitement (Phase 2) and Greed (Phase 3) are the most commonly skipped phases.**

If the protagonist goes from curiosity to fear without excitement/greed, the addiction loop breaks.

Force these two phases in the story design:
- "वाह! ये तो कमाल है। ये सच में हो रहा है।" (Excitement — after first confirmation)
- "बस एक बार और देख लूं। उसके बाद छोड़ दूंगा।" (Greed — after 2-3 repetitions)

**Show anticipation explicitly:**
```
✅ "मैं बिल्कुल बच्चों की तरह रात होने का इंतजार कर रहा था। वक्त बीत ही नहीं रहा था।"
✅ "मेरी नींद उड़ गई थी। मैं बार-बार घड़ी देख रहा था। अरे ये वक्त क्यों नहीं बीतता?"
```

## REAL-WORLD CONSEQUENCES

Every major event must produce a TANGIBLE effect outside the protagonist's mind:

| Event | Consequence |
|-------|-------------|
| First discovery | Protagonist loses sleep, makes a mistake at work |
| First test confirmed | Protagonist TELLS SOMEONE (coworker, friend) |
| Mechanism shows hidden info | Protagonist investigates physically — finds real evidence |
| Mechanism warns about danger | Something ACTUALLY HAPPENS (not just a feeling) |
| Moral dilemma choice | Tangible change: job lost, person hurt, police called |

```
✅ EXAMPLE 1:
- Protagonist publishes story online → PEOPLE RESPOND
- Police ARRIVE at his house
- He's taken to POLICE STATION
- He's shown a REAL BODY
- He CHANGES the story → letter-writer STOPS sending pages

✅ EXAMPLE 2:
- Painting prediction: arm wound → he actually CUTS his arm
- Painting shows treasure → he finds REAL SILVER COINS
- Painting shows shadow figure → he's actually IN DANGER
```

❌ **Never rely on "just a feeling" or "just in protagonist's head."**
❌ **Never have zero external evidence or witnesses.**

## TWIST ARCHITECTURE

### Valid Twist Types:

1. **Identity Swap** — X is actually Y (works for crime/mystery)
   - Example: "The letter-writer is the serial killer."

2. **Reality Anchor** — The mechanism is NOT supernatural
   - Example: "It was physically planted by someone."

3. **Observer Trap** — Protagonist is NOT who they think they are
   - Example: "You're not the guard. You're a PATIENT at this hospital."

### BANNED Twist Types (DO NOT USE):

- ❌ "It was all a dream / I was asleep the whole time."
- ❌ "I have a split personality / DID. I did it to myself."
- ❌ "I'm already dead / I'm a ghost."
- ❌ "It was me from the future / parallel universe me."
- ❌ "Everyone was in on it except me."
- ❌ "I'm in a mental institution. This is all in my head."
- ❌ "I was unconscious/had blackouts. I did everything in my sleep." (overused)

---

## TWIST FORESHADOWING RULES

A twist without foreshadowing is a CHEAT. The listener should feel "AH, of course!" not "Where did that come from?"

**3 Foreshadowing Rules:**

1. **Plant at least 2 clues in Act 1** — subtle, easy to miss, but OBVIOUS in retrospect

2. **Clues must be PHYSICAL** — an object, a line of dialogue, a behavior — not a feeling

3. **The twist character must APPEAR BEFORE the reveal** — no "mysterious helper revealed at the end" who was never shown

```
✅ GOOD FORESHADOWING (Francis twist):
  Act 1: Vijay mentions "फ्रांसिस को यहां तीस साल हो गए। वो कभी अपनी बात नहीं करता।"
         (Francis has been here 30 years. He never talks about himself.)
         → Plants: Francis is a fixture, has secrets.

  Act 1: "फ्रांसिस जब मेरी तरफ देखता है तो अजीब लगता है। जैसे वो कुछ जानता हो।"
         (When Francis looks at me, it's strange. Like he knows something.)
         → Plants: Francis has hidden knowledge.

  Act 2: "फ्रांसिस ने आज पूछा — 'विजय जी, आप ठीक हैं? आप रात को जागते हैं।'"
         (Francis asked: "Vijay ji, are you okay? You stay awake at night.")
         → Francis is watching Vijay. This matters later.

❌ BAD (no foreshadowing):
  Twist: Francis reveals he's Sunita's uncle and planted the bottle.
  Problem: Francis was barely mentioned in acts 1-2. No clues we missed.
  → Feels like deus ex machina.
```

## INTERNAL CONSISTENCY — LOGICAL GAPS

**This is the #1 killer of psychological horror. A single logical gap destroys ALL tension.**

### The Supernatural/Orchestrated Trap

When the mechanism does impossible things (label changes, notes appear, object moves), you have TWO possible explanations:

| Explanation | How it works | Risk |
|-------------|-------------|------|
| **Supernatural** | Spirit/force controls the object directly | Must have consistent RULES. Can't break own rules. |
| **Orchestrated** | A human (supporting character) is secretly manipulating the object | Must be PHYSICALLY POSSIBLE. No magic. |

**CRITICAL RULE: You MUST pick ONE explanation and STICK with it.**

```
❌ BROKEN: Bottle rolls by itself at 3:11am AND Francis says "I planted the bottle." 
   These contradict. If Francis controls it, how does it roll in a locked room? 
   If supernatural, Francis is irrelevant.

✅ FIX — Option A (Pure Supernatural):
   Make the bottle genuinely haunted by Sunita's spirit. Francis just made sure 
   Vijay FOUND the bottle. The bottle's powers are clearly supernatural with 
   established rules.

✅ FIX — Option B (Pure Orchestrated):
   Francis has a duplicate key. He enters the store room when Vijay is away. 
   He uses a thin wire to pull the bottle off the shelf. He swaps labels. 
   He plants notes. The bottle NEVER does anything that Francis couldn't 
   physically control.
```

### Consistency Checklist (for EACH mechanism event):

```
[ ] Does this event have a CLEAR trigger? (time, condition, action)
[ ] Is the trigger CONSISTENT with the mechanism's established rules?
[ ] If supernatural: does this event follow the rules OR break them (for escalation)?
[ ] If orchestrated: could a real person PHYSICALLY do this?
[ ] Does any character have CONTRADICTORY knowledge about the mechanism?
[ ] Is the mechanism's BEHAVIOR consistent across all 5-6 showings?
```
