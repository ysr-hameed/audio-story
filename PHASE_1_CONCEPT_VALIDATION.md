# PHASE 1: CONCEPT VALIDATION

**Purpose:** Filter bad concepts before investing time in story development.

**Read this phase FIRST. Do not proceed to Phase 2 until your concept passes all checks.**

---

## CRITICAL GENRE RULE

**This project produces PSYCHOLOGICAL HORROR / MYSTERY / THRILLER only.**

- ❌ NO emotional family drama, love stories, grief stories, relationship plots
- ❌ NO dead wife/husband/parent/child sending messages
- ❌ NO grieving protagonist, NO saving family members
- ❌ NO romantic interest as motivation
- ✅ Cold cognitive horror — listener feels UNEASE, not sadness
- ✅ High-IQ concept — listener can SOLVE the puzzle along with protagonist
- ✅ Professional setting (hotel, office, factory, hospital, toll plaza, station, warehouse) — NOT home/family

---

## INSTANT REJECTION RULES

If a story concept contains ANY of these, REJECT IT immediately:

### Family/Love (Absolute NO)
- Dead or dying spouse/family member as plot device
- Protagonist grieving a loss
- Love/relationship as primary motivation
- Saving a family member
- Messages from deceased loved ones
- Home/family setting as primary location

### Overused Twist Types
- "It was all a dream / coma / hallucination"
- "I have a split personality / DID"
- "I'm already dead / a ghost"
- "I was in a mental institution all along"
- "I have a condition and I do things in my sleep" (overused)
- "It was me from the future / alternate timeline"

### Mechanism Problems
- Ghost/spirit appears and talks directly (too easy, no mechanism)
- Dreams/visions as primary delivery (unreliable, undetectable)
- Protagonist is the ONLY one who can see/hear (no external validation)
- Generic object: "I found a diary" — must specify WHAT the diary DOES
- "I just knew" / intuition / sixth sense — not a mechanism

### Setting Problems
- All events occur in protagonist's home
- Family home or ancestral property
- Rural/village setting (prefer urban professional)
- School, college, hostel

---

## CONCEPT VALIDATION CHECKLIST

Answer these questions about your concept:

```
[ ] Is this psychological horror (NOT emotional drama)?
[ ] Is the setting professional/workplace (NOT home/family)?
[ ] Does the protagonist have NO spouse/children/parents mentioned?
[ ] Is the mechanism PHYSICAL (can be seen/verified by others)?
[ ] Is the twist NOT on the banned list?
[ ] Can the hook appear in the FIRST 300 WORDS?
[ ] Does the mechanism have clear RULES that can be tested?
[ ] Are there at least 3 supporting characters possible in this setting?
```

**If ANY answer is NO → STOP. Revise the concept or choose a different one.**


---

## HOOK TIMING RULE

⚠️ **CRITICAL: The hook (mystery/discovery) MUST appear within the first 300 words.**

```
❌ WRONG: 1,000 words of backstory → THEN mystery appears
✅ RIGHT: Hook in first 300 words → THEN backstory woven around it
```

**Structure:**
- **Lines 1-5:** Hook — protagonist encounters something strange
- **Lines 6-300:** Backstory woven into the hook — WHO is this person, WHY are they here
- **Lines 300-1,000:** Deepen character + first denial/dismissal of the mystery

Ask yourself: "Can I start with the protagonist discovering the mystery in the first paragraph?"
If not, simplify your concept.
